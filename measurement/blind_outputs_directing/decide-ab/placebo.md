Ship the 90%.

Three reasons:

1. **You have slack, not excess.** Deadline is Sunday. "One more day" almost never takes one day. If polish slips to two, you're shipping untested on deadline day or missing it entirely.

2. **The last 10% is where surprises live.** The 90% is known-good. The remaining gap is unscoped by definition—that's why it's still open. Polishing often uncovers issues you didn't plan for.

3. **Shipped beats perfect.** 90% in users' hands today gives you real feedback. The gap between 90% and 100% as *you* define it may not match what actually matters to them.

Ship today. Use the remaining time as buffer for bugs, or start the 100% push knowing you already have a fallback in the can.

One exception: if the missing 10% is a dealbreaker (security, data loss, core feature not working), polish. Otherwise, ship.
